Bohr Model Diagram + Formulas

  • Energy of nth orbit: Eₙ = −13.6 / n² eV
  • Energy gap: ΔE = |E𝒇 − Eᵢ|
  • Photon relation: ΔE = hf = hc / λ
  • Wavelength in nm: λ = 1240 / ΔE
  • Absorption: electron jumps from lower orbit to higher orbit.
  • Emission: electron falls from higher orbit to lower orbit and emits photon.

Simple Explanation

In Bohr model, electron can revolve only in fixed energy levels. It cannot stay between two orbits. To jump up, it absorbs exact energy. When it falls down, it emits photon energy equal to the difference between two energy levels.

NEET Traps

  • Energy levels are negative because electron is bound to nucleus.
  • Electron needs exact energy difference to jump up.
  • Emission happens when electron moves from higher n to lower n.
  • Lyman series ends at n = 1 and lies in ultraviolet region.
  • Balmer series ends at n = 2 and mostly lies in visible region.
