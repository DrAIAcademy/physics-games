Electric Field Diagram + Formulas

  • Electric field due to point charge: E = kq / r²
  • Force on charge in field: F = qE
  • Electric potential: V = kq / r
  • Net electric field is vector sum of fields due to all charges.
  • Field direction is the direction of force on a positive test charge.
  • Negative test charge feels force opposite to electric field direction.

Simple Explanation

Electric field is the region around a charge where another charge feels force. A positive source charge pushes positive test charge away, while a negative source charge attracts it. In this game, the test charge moves because of the net electric force from both source charges.

NEET Traps

  • Electric field direction is defined using a positive test charge.
  • Positive charge gives field outward; negative charge gives field inward.
  • Electric field is vector; electric potential is scalar.
  • At midpoint of equal opposite charges, electric field is not zero.
  • At midpoint of equal like charges, electric field can become zero.

Observation Questions

  1. What happens to electric field when distance from charge doubles?
  2. Why does a negative test charge move opposite to field direction?
  3. Why do field lines start from positive charge?
  4. Where can electric field become zero for two equal positive charges?
  5. Why is electric potential scalar but electric field vector?
