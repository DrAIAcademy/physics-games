Goal: make XL equal to XC. At resonance, impedance is minimum and current is maximum.

Formula Focus

  • Inductive reactance: XL = 2πfL
  • Capacitive reactance: XC = 1/(2πfC)
  • Impedance: Z = √[R² + (XL − XC)²]
  • Current: Irms = Vrms/Z
  • Resonance: XL = XC
  • Resonant frequency: f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)
  • Power factor: cosφ = R/Z

Simple NEET Explanation

In a series LCR circuit, inductor and capacitor oppose current in opposite ways. At resonance, their reactances become equal and cancel each other. Then the circuit behaves like a pure resistor and current becomes maximum.

In this game, the reactor powers up when your AC frequency is close to the resonant frequency.

NEET Traps

  • At resonance, impedance is minimum, not maximum.
  • At resonance, current is maximum.
  • At resonance, power factor is 1.
  • Current and voltage are in phase at resonance.
  • Inductor dominates at high frequency.
  • Capacitor dominates at low frequency.
