Formula Focus

  • Fission: heavy nucleus splits into two lighter nuclei + neutrons + energy.
  • Chain reaction: emitted neutrons cause further fission.
  • Critical reactor: neutron production rate = neutron loss rate.
  • Subcritical: reaction decreases with time.
  • Supercritical: reaction increases rapidly.
  • Control rods absorb neutrons.
  • Moderator slows neutrons to make fission more effective.

Simple NEET Explanation

A nuclear reactor works by controlled fission chain reaction. Neutrons released from one fission can cause more fissions. Moderator slows neutrons, control rods absorb extra neutrons, and coolant removes heat.

In this game, the mission is to keep the reactor close to critical condition without making it dangerously supercritical.

NEET Traps

  • Control rods reduce reaction by absorbing neutrons.
  • Moderator slows neutrons, it does not stop the reaction.
  • Coolant removes heat; it is not the same as moderator.
  • Critical does not mean dangerous; it means stable chain reaction.
  • Supercritical reaction can become dangerous if uncontrolled.
  • Fission is splitting; fusion is joining of light nuclei.
