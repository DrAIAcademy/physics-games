Formula Focus

  • Transformer voltage ratio: Vs/Vp = Ns/Np
  • Ideal transformer power: VpIp = VsIs
  • Step-up transformer: Ns > Np, so Vs > Vp
  • Step-down transformer: Ns < Np, so Vs < Vp
  • For ideal transformer: Is/Ip = Np/Ns
  • Efficiency: η = output power / input power
  • High voltage transmission reduces I²R loss.

Simple NEET Explanation

A transformer works on mutual induction. Changing current in the primary coil produces changing magnetic flux in the iron core, which induces emf in the secondary coil. The voltage depends on the turns ratio.

In this game, the city grid powers up when you choose a proper turns ratio for the required secondary voltage.

NEET Traps

  • Transformer works only with AC, not steady DC.
  • Step-up increases voltage but decreases current.
  • Step-down decreases voltage but increases current.
  • Power is nearly conserved in ideal transformer.
  • High voltage transmission reduces current and hence reduces I²R loss.
  • Turns ratio controls voltage ratio.
