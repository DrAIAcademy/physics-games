Collision Diagram + Formulas

  • Momentum: p = mv
  • Conservation of momentum: m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
  • Elastic collision: kinetic energy is conserved.
  • Perfectly inelastic collision: both bodies move together after collision.
  • Coefficient of restitution: e = speed of separation / speed of approach
  • Impulse changes momentum: J = Δp

Simple Explanation

In a collision, total momentum of the system remains conserved when no external force acts. In elastic collision, kinetic energy is also conserved. In inelastic collision, kinetic energy is lost into heat, sound or deformation, but momentum is still conserved.

NEET Traps

  • Momentum is conserved in both elastic and inelastic collisions.
  • Kinetic energy is conserved only in perfectly elastic collision.
  • In perfectly inelastic collision, objects stick together.
  • Velocity sign matters: opposite direction means negative velocity.
  • Coefficient of restitution is between 0 and 1 for normal collisions.
