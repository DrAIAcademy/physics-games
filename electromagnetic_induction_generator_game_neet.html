Formula Focus

  • Magnetic flux: Φ = BA cosθ
  • Faraday law: ε = −N dΦ/dt
  • Magnitude of induced emf: |ε| = N |dΦ/dt|
  • Lenz law: induced current opposes the cause of flux change.
  • More turns N gives more induced emf.
  • Faster motion/change gives greater induced emf.
  • No flux change means no induced emf.

Simple NEET Explanation

Electromagnetic induction occurs when magnetic flux linked with a coil changes. This change produces induced emf. Direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change in flux, according to Lenz law.

In this game, the station powers up when your coil-magnet setup generates emf close to the mission target.

NEET Traps

  • Constant magnetic flux gives zero induced emf.
  • Only change in flux matters, not just magnetic field presence.
  • Negative sign in Faraday law represents Lenz law.
  • More coil turns increase emf.
  • Induced current direction opposes the change causing it.
  • Maximum flux occurs when coil normal is parallel to B.
