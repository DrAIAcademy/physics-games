Formula Focus

  • Forward bias: p-side to positive, n-side to negative.
  • Reverse bias: p-side to negative, n-side to positive.
  • Silicon threshold ≈ 0.7 V.
  • Germanium threshold ≈ 0.3 V.
  • Approx. forward current: I = (V − Vth)/R.
  • Reverse current is almost zero before breakdown.
  • Zener diode works in reverse breakdown region for voltage regulation.

Simple NEET Explanation

A semiconductor diode allows current mainly in one direction. In forward bias, current flows after threshold voltage. In reverse bias, current is almost zero until breakdown. Zener diode is specially used in reverse breakdown for voltage regulation.

In this game, the electronic gate opens when the diode is biased correctly and the current/output condition matches the mission.

NEET Traps

  • Diode is not an ohmic conductor.
  • Forward resistance is low after threshold.
  • Reverse resistance is very high before breakdown.
  • Silicon threshold is about 0.7 V, germanium about 0.3 V.
  • Zener diode is reverse biased in voltage regulation.
  • LED must be forward biased and needs current limiting resistance.
