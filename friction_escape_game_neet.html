Free Body Diagram + Formulas

  • Component down the plane: mg sinθ
  • Normal reaction: N = mg cosθ
  • Friction force: f = μN
  • If trying to move upward: Net force = F − mg sinθ − μN
  • Acceleration: a = Fnet / m
  • Rover moves upward if applied force is greater than resisting forces.

Simple Explanation

The rover is on an inclined plane. Gravity pulls it down the slope by mg sinθ. Normal reaction is mg cosθ. Friction opposes motion and equals μN. To escape upward, the applied force must beat both gravity component and friction.

NEET Traps

  • Normal reaction on an incline is mg cosθ, not mg.
  • Downward component along the plane is mg sinθ.
  • Friction always opposes relative motion or tendency of motion.
  • Increasing angle increases mg sinθ and decreases mg cosθ.
  • Mass may cancel in some limiting friction angle cases, but not in every force problem.
