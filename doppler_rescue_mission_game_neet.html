Formula Focus

  • Doppler formula: f' = f × (v ± vₒ)/(v ∓ vₛ)
  • Observer moving toward source: numerator increases.
  • Source moving toward observer: denominator decreases.
  • Approaching motion → apparent frequency increases.
  • Receding motion → apparent frequency decreases.
  • Source wavelength in front: λfront = (v − vₛ)/f
  • Source wavelength behind: λback = (v + vₛ)/f

Simple NEET Explanation

Doppler effect is the apparent change in frequency due to relative motion between source and observer. If source and observer move closer, wavefronts get compressed and apparent frequency increases. If they move away, wavefronts spread out and apparent frequency decreases.

NEET Traps

  • Use sign convention carefully.
  • Frequency changes due to relative motion, not due to loudness.
  • Speed of sound depends on medium, not source speed.
  • Approaching source gives higher pitch.
  • Receding source gives lower pitch.
  • Doppler effect applies to sound and light, but formulas differ.
