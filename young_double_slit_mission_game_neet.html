Formula Focus

  • Fringe width: β = λD/d
  • Bright fringe: yₙ = nβ
  • Dark fringe: yₙ = (n + 1/2)β
  • Path difference: Δx = yd/D
  • Phase difference: Δφ = 2πΔx/λ
  • Increasing λ increases fringe width.
  • Increasing slit separation d decreases fringe width.

Simple NEET Explanation

Young's double slit experiment shows interference of light. Two coherent slits act as sources. Where waves meet in phase, bright fringes form. Where waves meet out of phase, dark fringes form.

In this game, your detector must be placed exactly at the selected bright or dark fringe position.

NEET Traps

  • Bright fringe path difference is nλ.
  • Dark fringe path difference is (n + 1/2)λ.
  • Fringe width increases when wavelength increases.
  • Fringe width decreases when slit separation increases.
  • Central bright fringe corresponds to n = 0.
  • Screen distance D directly affects fringe width.
