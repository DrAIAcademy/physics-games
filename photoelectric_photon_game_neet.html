Photoelectric Diagram + Formulas

  • Einstein photoelectric equation: hf = φ + Kmax
  • Maximum kinetic energy: Kmax = hf − φ
  • Emission condition: hf ≥ φ
  • Stopping potential: eVs = Kmax
  • Threshold frequency: f₀ = φ / h
  • Intensity affects photocurrent, not maximum kinetic energy.

Simple Explanation

When light falls on a metal surface, electrons are emitted only if the light frequency is high enough. This minimum frequency is called threshold frequency. Higher intensity sends more photons, so more electrons can be emitted, but intensity alone cannot cause emission if frequency is below threshold.

NEET Traps

  • Below threshold frequency, no electron emission occurs even if intensity is high.
  • Intensity affects number of emitted electrons, not their maximum kinetic energy.
  • Frequency affects kinetic energy and stopping potential.
  • Stopping potential increases when frequency increases.
  • Emission is almost instantaneous once threshold condition is satisfied.
