Circuit Diagram + Formulas

  • Ohm's Law: V = IR
  • Current: I = V / R
  • Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
  • Series resistance: Rs = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ...
  • Parallel resistance: 1/Rp = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ + 1/R₃ + ...
  • In series, current is same. In parallel, voltage is same.

Simple Explanation

Ohm's Law connects voltage, current and resistance. Voltage pushes charges, resistance opposes current, and current is the flow of charge. In this game, your mission is to build a circuit that gives the target current needed to power the robot city safely.

NEET Traps

  • Ammeter is connected in series, voltmeter is connected in parallel.
  • In series combination, current remains same but voltage divides.
  • In parallel combination, voltage remains same but current divides.
  • Equivalent resistance in series is greater than each resistor.
  • Equivalent resistance in parallel is less than the smallest resistor.

Observation Questions

  1. What happens to current when voltage is doubled?
  2. What happens to current when resistance is doubled?
  3. Which quantity is same in series circuit?
  4. Which quantity is same in parallel circuit?
  5. Why is parallel equivalent resistance smaller?
