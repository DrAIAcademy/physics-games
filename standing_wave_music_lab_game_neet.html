Formula Focus

  • Wave speed: v = fλ
  • String fixed at both ends: fₙ = nv/2L
  • Open pipe: fₙ = nv/2L
  • Closed pipe: fₙ = nv/4L, where n = 1, 3, 5...
  • Node: point of zero displacement.
  • Antinode: point of maximum displacement.
  • Standing wave forms by superposition of two opposite travelling waves.

Simple NEET Explanation

Standing waves are formed when two waves of same frequency and amplitude travel in opposite directions and superpose. Some points remain still called nodes, and some vibrate maximum called antinodes.

In this game, resonance is locked when your driving frequency matches the allowed natural frequency of the string or pipe.

NEET Traps

  • Closed pipe has only odd harmonics.
  • Open pipe supports all harmonics.
  • String fixed at both ends has nodes at both ends.
  • Closed pipe has node at closed end and antinode at open end.
  • Frequency increases when length decreases.
  • Frequency increases when wave speed increases.
