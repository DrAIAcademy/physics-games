Formula Focus

  • Lens formula: 1/f = 1/v − 1/u
  • Cartesian convention: object on left → u is negative.
  • Magnification: m = v/u = h'/h
  • Power of lens: P = 1/f in metre
  • Convex lens: f positive. Concave lens: f negative.
  • Real image forms on opposite side and is inverted.
  • Virtual image forms on same side and is erect.

Simple NEET Explanation

A convex lens converges parallel rays and can form real or virtual images depending on object position. A concave lens diverges rays and usually forms a virtual, erect and diminished image.

In this game, your mission is to place the screen at the real image position. If the image is virtual, a physical screen cannot catch it.

NEET Traps

  • Convex lens does not always form a real image.
  • If object is between F and O, convex lens forms virtual, erect, magnified image.
  • Ray through optical centre goes undeviated.
  • Screen can catch only real images, not virtual images.
  • For power, focal length must be in metre.
  • Magnification sign tells orientation.
